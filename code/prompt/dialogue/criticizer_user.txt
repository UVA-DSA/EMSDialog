Hierarchical Definition of Topic Flow
Topic
 - Micro-Intent
    * step

0. Dispatch
    - radio dispatch
1. Introduction
    - introduction
2. Chief Complaint
    - identify primary complaint
3. Responsiveness Exam
    - AVPU
    - eye opening
    - verbal response
    - motor response 
4. Primary Assessment
    - check airway
        * Airway clearance
        * Airway: Patent, maintainable, un-maintainable
        * Preferred posture to maintain airway
        * Sounds of obstruction
    - check breathing
        * Rate and depth of respirations
        * Cyanosis
        * Position of the trachea
        * Work of breathing
        * Flaring of nostrils
        * Presence of bilateral breath sounds
        * Presence of adventitious breath sounds
        * Asymmetric chest movements
        * Oxygen saturation measured with pulse oximetry
    - check circulation
        * Pulse rate and quality
        * Skin appearance: Color
        * Peripheral pulses
        * Skin temperature
        * Urinary output
        * Blood Pressure
5. History of Present Illness
    - S.A.M.P.L.E.
      * signs and symptoms
      * allergies
      * medications
      * pertinent past medical history
      * last oral intake
      * events leading up to the event
    - collect patient personal information
6. Pain Assessment
    - O.P.Q.R.S.T.
      * onset
      * provocation
      * quality
      * radiation
      * severity
      * time
7. Secondary Assessment
    - skin exam
        * Presence of petechia, purpura, abrasions,
        * bruises, scars, or birthmarks
        * Bite / Sting marks
        * Rashes
        * Abnormal skin turgor
        * Temperature
        * Color: Jaundice, pallor, etc.
    - head and neck exam
        * Pupillary reflex / Size of pupils
        * Gross visual examination
        * Abnormal extra-ocular movements
        * Assessment of mental status / Short & Long
        * term memory assessment
        * Neck veins
        * Swallowing difficulties
        * Nuchal rigidity
        * Presence of lymphadenopathy or neck masses
        * Scars
    - ears, nose, mouth and throat exam
        * Hemorrhage
        * Drainage
        * Sunken eyes
        * Gross assessment of the hearing
        * Obstruction
        * Foreign body
    - thorax/lungs/cardio exam
        * Breath sounds
        * Heart Sounds
        * Peripheral vs. Central Pulse Comparison
        * Scars
    - abdomen exam
        * Shape and size
        * Bowel sounds
        * Tenderness / Rigidity / Guarding
        * Masses (i.e., suprapubic masses)
        * Pelvic tenderness, crepitus, or instability
        * Scars
    - genitourinary exam
        * Rectal bleeding 
        * Color of urine 
        * Frequency / Urgency of urination
        * Stools – Normal / Color
    - extremities and back exam
        * Gross motor and sensory function
        * Peripheral pulses 
        * Lack of use of an extremity 
        * Deformity, angulation 
        * Wounds, abrasions
        * Vertebral column, flank, buttocks
8. Take Vital Signs
    - pulse
    - resp
    - bp
    - glucose
    - spo2
    - ekg
9. Take Interventions
    - give medications
    - take procedures
10. Exit to Protocol:
    - decide ems protocol
11. Reassessments:
    - retake vital signs
        * pulse
        * resp
        * bp
        * glucose
        * spo2
        * ekg
    - retake interventions 
        * give medications
        * take procedures
    - redo pain assessment
        * onset
        * provocation
        * quality
        * radiation
        * severity
        * time
    - redo history of present illness
      * signs and symptoms
      * allergies
      * medications
      * pertinent past medical history
      * last oral intake
      * events leading up to the event
    * collect patient personal information
12. Transport
    - destination decision
    - movement secured
